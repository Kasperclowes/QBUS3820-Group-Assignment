Problem Statement: 

Which customers are about to stop shopping with us? Without a model, the retailer has no way of
knowing a customer is drifting away until it is too late to act. By predicting which customers are
unlikely to shop in the next few weeks, the retailer can send a targeted retention offer to bring them
back, saving the cost of acquiring a new customer. 
Variables to look at:
• how recently a customer last shopped
• how frequently they shopped in the past
• whether their visit frequency has been declining over time
• demographic information like income and household size


REMARKS on train/validation/test data: 

- When creating training/validation split, split in resepct of diffferent houshold ids.  
This is important for good generalisation of the model, so the model doesnt recognise a houshold from the training data 
- Aim for 70 % training, 15 % validation and 15 % test data. 

Reformulated task:  

How would we distinguish between customer groups, to determine which customers are about to leave. 
Given that we do not have distinct customer ids, only houshold ids, 
The ML model's task (most likely a random forest with a classifier output) would be 
to use a set of inputs, including a specific houshold id to predict if the houshold id is about to churn in the next few weeks (number),
